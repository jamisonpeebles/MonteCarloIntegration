\section{Abstract}

The paper written by William Bridges and Neil Calkin, \textit{Log transformations: What not to expect when you're expecting} provides an investigation into an interesting phenomenon concerning the problematic behavior of the lognormal distribution. 
When sampled with a large value of its standard deviations, the lognormal distribution produces extremely large values, making it incredibly difficult to determine its summary statistics using sampling methods.
In my investigation, I will attempt to replicate the results of Bridges and Calkin, and then furher investigat this strange behavior of the lognormal distribution by looking at how the sample mean and sample median behave as the standard deviation increases.
My primary methods will include sampling from the lognormal distribution with a large standard deviation, and then calculating the sample mean and sample median for each sample.
By visualizing the results, I hope to gain a better understanding of how the lognormal distribution behaves with large standard deviations, and how this behavior might be modeled to better understand the underlying phenomena.
